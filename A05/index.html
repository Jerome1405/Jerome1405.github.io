Welcome to Barcelona

Barcelona, the cosmopolitan capital of Spain’s Catalonia region, is known for its art and architecture. The fantastical Sagrada Família church and other modernist landmarks designed by Antoni Gaudí dot the city. Museu Picasso and Fundació Joan Miró feature modern art by their namesakes. City history museum MUHBA includes several Roman archaeological sites.

Explore the City

Discover the beauty, history, and passion of this amazing city. Barcelona is full of vibrant culture, stunning architecture, and delicious cuisine.

Sagrada Familia

Iconic cathedral under construction, showcasing breathtaking stained glass windows and intricate architecture. Features include audio guides, tower access for panoramic views, and a rich history.

Gothic Quarter

Historic neighborhood with a labyrinth of narrow streets, home to impressive architecture like the local cathedral and the Pont del Bisbe, filled with quaint shops and cafes.

Casa Batlló

Modernist architecture gem with whimsical design, featuring unique lighting effects and an augmented reality tour. Includes audio guide for historical insight.

Park Güell

Mosaic-adorned park on a hill featuring unique architecture and nature. Ideal for fans of whimsical design and historical narratives, with a serene ambiance.

Mercat de la Boqueria

Officially known as the Mercat de San Josep as it was built on a former monastery, La Boqueria is a popular food market along Barcelona's touristy La Rambla stretch.

Palace of Catalan Music

Art Nouveau concert hall with intricate mosaics, stained glass, and a remarkable skylight. Hosts music performances and art exhibits, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
